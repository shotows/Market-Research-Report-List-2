<p><p>Gas detector alarms can be categorized into two main types, toxic gas alarms and combustible gas alarms. Toxic gas alarms are designed to detect and alert individuals to the presence of harmful gases such as carbon monoxide, ammonia, or chlorine. Combustible gas alarms, on the other hand, are used to detect the presence of flammable gases like methane, propane, or butane. Both types of alarms play a critical role in ensuring the safety of occupants in industrial, commercial, and residential settings by providing early warning of potentially dangerous gas leaks.</p></p>
