<p><p>Rocket packs can be classified into two market types based on the type of propellant they use: gases and liquids. </p><p>In the gases market, rocket packs are powered by compressed air, nitrogen, or other gases to generate thrust. These packs are typically lighter and more compact, making them suitable for shorter duration flights or smaller payloads.</p><p>In the liquids market, rocket packs utilize liquid fuels like hydrogen and oxygen or kerosene and liquid oxygen to create combustion and propulsion. These packs offer greater power and longer flight times, making them ideal for larger payloads or longer missions.</p></p>
