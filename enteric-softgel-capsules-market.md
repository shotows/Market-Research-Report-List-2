<p><p>Enteric softgel capsules can be found in two main types: gelatin and vegetable. Gelatin capsules are made from animal products, primarily collagen from bovine or porcine sources. On the other hand, vegetable capsules are derived from plant-based materials such as cellulose or starch. Both types offer the same benefits of enteric coating, which helps protect the contents of the capsule from stomach acid, ensuring better absorption in the intestines. These two market types cater to different consumer preferences, including those with dietary restrictions or ethical considerations.</p></p>
