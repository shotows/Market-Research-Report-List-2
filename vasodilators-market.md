<p><p>Vasodilators are medications that widen blood vessels, improving blood flow and reducing blood pressure. There are three main types of vasodilators on the market: Arterial dilators, which mainly target arteries to lower blood pressure; Venous dilators, which primarily act on veins to reduce the amount of blood returning to the heart; and Mixed dilators, which work on both arteries and veins. Each type of vasodilator has its own unique mechanism of action and therapeutic benefits for various cardiovascular conditions.</p></p>
